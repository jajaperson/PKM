# Perfect electric dipole

A **perfect electric dipole** with [[electric dipole moment]] $\vab p$ is an idealized charge distribution in electrostatics whose [[Multipole expansion of the electrostatic potential]] has all terms vanish except for the dipole term.
Thus the potential due to a perfect dipole located at $\vab r'$ is
$$
\begin{align*}
V(\vab r) = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_{0}} \frac{\vab p \cdot \unitv \SR}{\Sr^2}
\end{align*}
$$
and given a continuous distribution of perfect dipoles $\vab P$ localized to $\Omega$
$$
\begin{align*}
V(\vab r) = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_{0}} \iiint_{\Omega} \frac{\vab P(\vab r') \cdot \unitv \SR}{\Sr^2}\,d\tau'
\end{align*}
$$
A perfect dipole arises by considering a physical dipole $\vab p = q\vab d$ in the limit as $\vab d \to \vab 0$ and $q \to \infty$ so that $\vab p$ remains fixed.
