# $\mathcal K$-tensor product of modules

Let $M, N$ be [[Module over a commutative ring|modules over a commutative ring]] $\mathcal{K}$.
The **tensor product** $M \otimes_{\mathcal{K}} N$ is an $\mathcal{K}$-module such that the $\mathcal{K}$-[[Multilinear map|bilinear]] maps from $M \times N$ are in correspondence with the $\mathcal{K}$-[[Module homomorphism|linear]] maps from $M \otimes_{\mathcal{K}} N$, as defined by the [[#Universal property]].[^2009] 
A generalization for the [[Tensor product of modules over a noncommutative ring]] exists, but is not necessarily a module.
We recover this notion of tensor product as the [[Tensor product of modules over a noncommutative ring#Tensor product of bimodules]].

## Universal property

Let $M, N$ be $\mathcal{K}$-modules.
The tensor product is a pair consisting of an $\mathcal{K}$-module $M \otimes_{\mathcal{K}} N$ together with an $\mathcal{K}$-[[Multilinear map|bilinear]] map $(\otimes) : M \times N \to M \otimes_{\mathcal{K}} N$
such any $\mathcal{K}$-bilinear map $\varphi : M \times N \to P$ factorizes uniquely through $(\otimes)$ #m/def/module/comm

## Construction

Let $R^{(M \times N)}$ be the [[free module]] on $M \times N$ with the natural inclusion function $\iota: M \times N \hookrightarrow R^{(M \times N)}$.
Let $K$ denote the $\mathcal{K}$-[[Submodule]] of $R^{(M \times N)}$ generated by elements of the form
$$
\begin{align*}
\iota(m, \alpha n_{1} + \beta n_{2})  - \alpha\iota(m,n_{1}) - \beta\iota(m, n_{2}); \\
\iota(\alpha m_{1} + \beta m_{2}, n) - \alpha\iota(m_{1},n) - \beta\iota(m_{2},n);
\end{align*}
$$
for all $m,m_{1},m_{2} \in M$, $n,n_{1},n_{2} \in N$, $\alpha,\beta \in R$.
We construct the tensor product as the [[quotient module]]
$$
\begin{align*}
M \otimes_{\mathcal{K}} N = \mathcal{K}^{(M \times N)} / K
\end{align*}
$$
with its natural projection $\pi : \mathcal{K}^{(M \times N)} \twoheadrightarrow M \otimes_{\mathcal{K}} N$,
so that the map
$$
\begin{align*}
(\otimes) = \pi \circ \iota : M \times N \to M \otimes_{\mathcal{K}} N
\end{align*}
$$
