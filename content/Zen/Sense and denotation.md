# Sense and denotation
Given a logical sentence, there are two opposing ways of viewing it

- **As its sense** (dynamic, syntactic)
  — as the sequence of instructions encoded in syntax.
  - $A \lor B$ instructs a [[Disjunction]] between $A$ and $B$
- **As its denotation** (infinite, static)
  — as its *idealised* final result.
  - $1 + 5$ _denotes_ the same thing as $6$

Two sentences having the same _denotation_ implies they have the same _sense_,
but the converse is not necessarily true
