# Invariant set

In a [[dynamical system]] an **invariant set** $A$ is a subset of the [[phase space]] closed under [[Trajectory|time evolution]],
i.e. $\Phi^t \mathbf{x}_{0} \in A$ for all $\mathbf{x}_{0} \in A$ and all $t$[^domain]. #m/def/dynamics 

[^domain]: with $t \in \mathbb{R}$ in the continuous case and $t \in \mathbb{Z}$ or $\mathbb{N}$ in the discrete case.

- A **positively invariant set** is invariant under forward evolution
- A **negatively invariant set** is invariant under reverse evolution

## Examples

- [[Fixed point]]
- Every trajectory $\Phi^\mathbb{R} \mathbf{x}_{0}$ is an invariant set
- Separatrix 
- [[Basin of attraction]]
- Constant manifold for a [[Conserved quantity]]
- [[𝛼- and 𝜔-limit sets]]
