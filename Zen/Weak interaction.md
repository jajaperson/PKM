[[Fundamental interactions]]
# Weak interaction
The **weak interaction** is a fundamental interaction primarily involved in kinds of atomic decay.
Here the distinction between _force_ and _interaction_ becomes most apparent:
there is no force operating here as such,
although an exchange of energy and momentum occurs,
and thus an interaction.
Virtual $\ce{ W+, W-, \& Z^0 }$ bosons are present as intermediaries in processes such as $\ce{ \beta+ }$- and $\beta^-$-decay
$$
\begin{align*}
\ce{ n -> p+ + W- -> &p+ + e- + \overline{\nu_{e}}} \\
\ce{ p+ -> n + W+ -> &n + e+ + \nu_{e}}
\end{align*}
$$
Note the second process never occurs for a free proton,
since these are stable.
$\ce{ W+ }$ and $W^-$ are antiparticles of each other.
