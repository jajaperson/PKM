# Yukawa potential
The **Yukawa potential** is a generalised or "screened" [[Electric potential of a point charge|Coulomb potential]]
$$
\begin{align*}
V(r) = \kappa \frac{e^{-mcr/\hbar}}{r}
\end{align*}
$$
Where $m:\pu{ kg }$ is the mass of an interaction's mediating particle.
Note that when $m = 0$ the potential $V(r) = \frac{\kappa}{r}$, 
which is in the form of the coulomb potential,
explaining why the potential for the [[Electromagnetic interaction]] is of this form —
[[photon|photons]], the mediating particle for this interaction, are indeed massless.
It follows that
$$
\begin{align*}
\kappa_{\gamma} = \frac{q_{1}q_{2}}{4\pi\epsilon_{0}}
\end{align*}
$$
The exponential decay introduced by any $m>0$ explains the short range of the [[Weak interaction]].
