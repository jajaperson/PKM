# Unitary representation

A **unitary representation** $\mathfrak{X}$ of $G$ is a [[group homomorphism]] $\mathfrak{X} : G \to \opn{U}(V)$ into the [[unitary group]] #m/def/rep2
where $V$ is a complex [[inner product space]].
Thus $\mathfrak{X}$ is a [[group representation]] carried by $V$ such that
$$
\begin{align*}
\braket{ \mathfrak{X}(g)v | \mathfrak{X}(g)w } = \braket{ v | w } 
\end{align*}
$$
for all $g \in G$ and $v,w \in V$ where $\braket{ - | - }$ is the inner product.

## Properties

- [[Every finite complex representation of a compact group is equivalent to a unitary representation]].
- [[The orthogonal complement of an invariant subspace under a unitary operator is invariant]]
