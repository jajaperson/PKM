[[Thermodynamics MOC]]
# Work
Work is the _mechanical_ transfer of energy due to a force acting over a displacement, i.e.
$$
\begin{align*}
\dj W &= \vab F \cdot d\vab s
\end{align*}
$$
Work differs from [[Heat]] in that it does not require a temperature difference to be transferred.
However, like heat, work can result in a change in temperature —
stirring a cup of coffee _will_ increase the $E_k$ of constituent particles
and therefore increase the temperature,
but it would be incorrect to say the coffee has been heated.

See [[Quasistatic expression for work]].


The convention is positive work indicated work done _by_ a system.
