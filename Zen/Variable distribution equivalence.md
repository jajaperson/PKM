[[Cumulative distribution function]]
# Variable distribution equivalence
An [[Equivalence relation]] $(\sim)$ may be defined on [[Real random variable|random variables]] that share the same distribution,
such that
$$
\begin{align*}
X \sim Y \iff \forall t \in \mathbb{R} \quad F_{X}(t) = F_{Y}(t)
\end{align*}
$$
It is immediately clear that this forms an equivalence relation.
This is useful since it allows the modelling of one random variable using another one.
Particularly, it is common to compare a random variable to archetypal distributions in this way,
e.g. $X \sim \Bin(n,p)$.
