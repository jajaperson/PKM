# Indistinguishable particles

If two particles with states described by Hilbert spaces are instinguishable,
each described by an identical Hilbert space $\mathcal{H}$,
then the action of $S_{2}$ on $\mathcal{H}^{\otimes 2}$ must at most change the global phase of their combined state;
i.e. $\ket{\psi}$ transforms in either the [[Alternating character|alternating irrep]] or trivial irrep.

- $S_{2}$ commutes with Hamiltonian, thus particles will remain in one of these irreps forever.
- It turns out that antisymmetric particles have half-integer spin (fermions), symmetric particles have integer spin (bosons). This result is relativistic.
- [[Pauli exclusion principle]].
