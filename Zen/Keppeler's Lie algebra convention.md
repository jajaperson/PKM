[[Complex matrix Lie algebra]]
# Keppeler's matrix Lie algebra convention

In his course [[MAT-65-05|Groups and Representations]], [[Stefan Keppeler]] uses a slightly unorthodox convention of multiplying tangential matrices by $-i$ when forming the [[Complex matrix Lie algebra]][^kep], so given a Lie group $G \sube \mathrm{GL}_{n}(\mathbb{C})$
$$
\begin{align*}
\mathfrak{g} = \{ -ic'(0) : c \in \Man^\omega(\mathbb{R}, G) , c(0) = \mathbf{1}_{n} \} \sube \mathbb{C}^{n \times n}
\end{align*}
$$
Then the Lie bracket becomes the matrix commutator premultiplied by $-i$.
When used in these notes, I will reserve the brackets for the commutator and explicitly prepend the $-i$.

[^kep]: 2023, [[@keppelerGroupsRepresentations2023|Groups and representations]], §6.4, p. 82
