# Heavy syllable
A **heavy syllable** is a syllable containing more than one [[mora|morae]].
Inversely, a light syllable is a syllable of one mora,
while a **superheavy syllable** contains at least three.

Generally, this means the [[rime]] of the syllable branches,
but this depends on whether the coda functions as moraic within the given language.

## Examples
- In **Japanese**, any syllable containing the moraic nasal _ん_ <span class="broad">N</span> is necessarily heavy,
  as well as the syllable preceding a geminated consonant.
