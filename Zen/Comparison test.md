[[Tests for series divergence]]
# Comparison test
The **comparison test** for divergence of infinite series is analogous to the [[Squeeze Theorem]].
It states that if sequences $(a_{n})_{n=1}^\infty$ and $(b_{n})_{n=1}^\infty$ obey the relationship
$$
\begin{align*}
0 \leq a_{n} \leq b_{n}
\end{align*}
$$
for all $n \in \mathbb{N}$, then the series obey the relation
$$
\begin{align*}
0 \leq \sum_{n=1}^\infty a_{n} \leq \sum_{n=0}^\infty b_{n}
\end{align*}
$$
and therefore the convergence of the latter series implies the convergence of the former,
and the divergence of the former series implies the divergence of the former.

Often a series will be compared with a [[𝑝-series]].
