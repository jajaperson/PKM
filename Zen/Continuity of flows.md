# Continuity of flows

Let $F : \mathbb{R}^n \to\mathbb{R}^n$ be [[Differentiability|differentiable]].
Then for each $x_{0}\in \mathbb{R}^n$
there exists $T > 0$ and a neighbourhood $U$ of $x_{0}$
such that $\dot{x} = F(x)$ has [[Continuity|continuous]] solutions $q(t,x)$ for $t \in (-T,T)$ and $x \in U$ with $q(0,x)=x$.[^2021]
