[[Monoidal internalization]]
# Hopf monoid object

A <dfn>Hopf monoid</dfn> $\mathcal{H}$ in a [[symmetric monoidal category]] $\cat C$ is a [[Bimonoid object|bimonoid]] with the additional structure of an <dfn>antipous</dfn>,[^1] 
an endomorphism $\sigma : \mathcal{H} \to \mathcal{H}$ for which

![[antipodal-string.svg#invert|c]]

^antipous

holds.
The antipous law states precisely that the antipous is the [[Convolution monoid|convolution]]-inverse of the identity map,
whence such a map is unique when it exists.
In a [[cartesian category]] the antipous law is equivalent to the inverse law of a [[group object]], 
so that Hopf monoids are naturally viewed as a generalization of groups.
