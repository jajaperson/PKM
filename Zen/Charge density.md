# Charge Density
**Charge density** is a measure of how [[Charge]] is distributed over space.
Like [[Mass Density]] and other forms of density, 
it comes in three forms

| Type                   | Symbol and formula[^symb]   | SI unit      |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------- | ------------ |
| Volume charge density  | $\rho_q = \frac{dQ}{dV}$    | $\pu{C.m-3}$ |
| Surface charge density | $\sigma_q = \frac{dQ}{dA}$  | $\pu{C.m-2}$ |
| Linear charge density  | $\lambda_q = \frac{dQ}{d\ell}$ | $\pu{C.m-1}$ |

[^symb]: In situations where the type of density being referred to is clear,
	the subscript may be omitted.

These can all be integrated to ascertain a quantity of **total contained charge**
within a solid, surface, or line respectively.
In the solid case
$$
\begin{align*}
Q_\Omega = \iiint_\Omega{\rho_q \,dV}
\end{align*}
$$
