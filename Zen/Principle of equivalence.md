[[Category theory MOC]]
# Principle of equivalence


## In category theory

In category theory the principle says that properties of categories should be invariant under [[equivalence of categories]], which has as a consequence

> All grammatically correct properties of objects of a fixed category are to be invariant under isomorphism.[^1998]

Those properties which violate the principle are called <dfn>evil</dfn>.
Thus, unless a category is [[Univalent category|univalent]], it is evil to refer to equality of objects rather than isomorphism of objects.

[^1998]: 1998\. [[Sources/@makkaiCategoricalFoundationMathematics1998|Towards a Categorical Foundation of Mathematics]]
