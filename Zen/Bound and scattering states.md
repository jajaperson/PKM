# Bound and scattering states

A **bound state** is an energy eigenstate (i.e. [[Schrödinger equation|stationary state]]) where a particle is confined by a potential wall,
i.e. $E < V(\pm \infty)$.
A **scattering state** is one not confined by such a potential.

## Properties

1. Bound states correspond to situations where a particle would be classically bound within a region of finite volume 
  (although quantum mechanically the particle may be found outside this region).
2. Bound states are normalizable and quantized, whereas scattering states are only [[Dirac orthonormality|Dirac normalizable]] and continuous.
