# $\mathcal{K}$-bimonoid

Let $\mathcal{K}$ be a commutative ring.
A <dfn>$\mathcal{K}$-bimonoid</dfn> is a [[Bimonoid object|bimonoid]] in [[Category of modules over a commutative ring]], #m/def/ralg/hopf 
and thus at once a [[R-monoid]] and [[R-comonoid]] satisfying the [[Bimonoid object#^compat]], 
which in [[R-comonoid#Sweedler notation]] read
$$
\begin{align*}
\sum_{(xy)} (xy)_{(1)} \otimes (xy)_{(2)} &= \sum_{(x)} \sum_{(y)}x_{(1)} y_{(1)} \otimes x_{(2)} y_{(2)}; \\
\Delta(\eta) &= \eta \otimes \eta; \\
\epsilon(x y) &= \epsilon(x) \epsilon(y); \\
\epsilon \eta &= 1.
\end{align*}
$$
