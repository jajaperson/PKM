# Conserved quantity

In dynamics, a **conserved quantity** is any function of state $g(\mathbf{x})$ that remains constant for all time, #m/def/dynamics
i.e. $\dot{g}=0$ for a [[differential system]] or $g_{n+1} = g_{n}$ for an [[iterated map]].

## Properties

- One is guaranteed that the time evolution of any [[trajectory]] will remain on the manifold defined by $g(\mathbf{x}) = g(\mathbf{x}_{0})$,
  i.e. such manifolds are [[Invariant set|invariant sets]].
