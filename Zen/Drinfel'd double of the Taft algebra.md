# Drinfel'd double of the Taft algebra

For $n \geq 2$ an integer, suppose $\mathbb{K}$ has a primitive $n$th [[Cyclotomic field|root of unity]] $q \in \mathbb{K}$.
The [[Drinfel'd double]] $\mathcal{D}_{n}$ of the [[Taft algebra]] $\mathcal{H}_{n}$ can be explicitly presented as the $n^4$-dimensional [[Hopf algebra]] generated as a [[K-monoid]] by $\{ a,b,c,d \}$ under relations #m/thm/ralg/hopf 
$$
\begin{align*}
ba &= qab,
&
db &= qbd,
&
ca &= qac,
&
dc &= qcd,
\\
bc &= cb,
&
da-qad &= 1 - bc,
&
a^n &= 0 = d^n,
&
b^n &= 1 = c^n
\end{align*}
$$
with coproduct and coünit
$$
\begin{align*}
\Delta(a) &= a \otimes b + 1 \otimes a,
&
\Delta(d) &= d \otimes c + 1 \otimes d,
\\
\Delta(b) &= b \otimes b,
&
\Delta(c) &= c \otimes c,
\\
\epsilon (a) &= 0 = \epsilon(d),
&
\epsilon (b) &= 1 = \epsilon(c)
\end{align*}
$$
and antipous of order $2n$
$$
\begin{align*}
\sigma(a) &= -ab^{-1},
&
\sigma(b) &= b^{-1},
&
\sigma(c) &= c^{-1},
&
\sigma(d) &= -dc^{-1}
\end{align*}
$$
The standard basis is
$$
\begin{align*}
\{ a^m b^s c^t d^\ell :\mid: 0 \leq m, s, t, \ell < n \}.
\end{align*}
$$
The $n^2$-dimensional $\mathbb{K}$-submonoid generated by $\{ a,b \}$ is the Taft algebra $\mathcal{H}_n$.[^2000]

[^2000]: 2000\. [[Sources/@chenIrreducibleRepresentationsClass2000|Irreducible representations of a class of quantum doubles]], §1.

## Representation theory

The non-isomorphic [[Simple module|simple modules]] $V(\ell, r)$ for $\mathcal{D}_{n}$ are indexed by pairs $(\ell,r)$ where $1 \leq \ell \leq n$ is an integer and $r \in \mathbb{Z} / n \mathbb{Z}$.^[Ibid., §2.]
Here, $V(\ell, r)$ is an $\ell$-dimensional $\mathbb{K}$-space with basis $\{ v_{1}, \dots, v_{\ell} \}$
and $\mathcal{D}_{n}$-action defined by
$$
\begin{align*}
a v_{i} &= 
\begin{cases}
v_{i+1} & 1 \leq i < \ell \\
0 & i = \ell
\end{cases},
&
d v_{i} &=
\begin{cases}
0 & i = 1 \\
\alpha_{i-1}(\ell)v_{i-1} & 1 < i \leq \ell
\end{cases}
,
\\
bv_{i} &= q^{r + i - 1}v_{i},
&
cv_{i} &= q^{i-r-\ell}v_{i}
\end{align*}
$$
for $i = 1, \dots, \ell$, where[^1]
$$
\begin{align*}
\alpha_{i}(\ell)
&= (i)_{q} (1-q^{i-\ell}) = \frac{(q^i-1)(1-q^{i-\ell})}{q-1}.
\end{align*}
$$

[^1]: See [[q-analogue]].
