[[Hierarchy of spectral lines]]
# Gross structure

**Gross structure** refers to the most basic level of structure in the energy levels in an atom,
due to the electron-nucleus interaction, electron kinetic energy, and electron-electron repulsion.
These are described by the [[Electric potential of a point charge|Coulomb potential]] and electron-electron repulsion (the residual electrostatic interaction).
Transitions between gross structure levels are of the order of an electronvolt and produce radiation in the visible range.

In spectroscopy, gross structure transitions are labeled by symbols of the form $\ce{ H }n \alpha$ where $n$ is the lower level's principal quantum number, and $\alpha$ is the $\Delta n$th Greek letter where $\Delta n$ is the difference between two levels.
For example, $\ce{ He }109\delta$ is the $109 \to 113$ transition in Helium.
