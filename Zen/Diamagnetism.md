# Diamagnetism
Unlike the other types of substantial magnetism ([[Ferromagnetism]], [[Paramagnetism]]),
diamagnetism occurs in the absence of unpaired electrons (in fact in all substances),
and the induced magnetic moments will _oppose_ an external magnetic field,
leading the substance to be repelled by a magnet.
This is however very weak when compared to the other two forms of magnetism,
and as such a very strong magnet is usually required to achieve visible effects.
