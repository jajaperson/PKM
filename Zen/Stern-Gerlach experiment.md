# Stern-Gerlach experiment

The Stern-Gerlach experiment involves passing a beam of spin-$\frac{1}{2}$ particles through an inhomogeneous magnetic field
$$
\begin{align*}
\mathbf{B}(x,y,z) = -\alpha x \mathbf{i} + (B_{0} + \alpha z) \mathbf{k}
\end{align*}
$$
experiencing a force
$$
\begin{align*}
\mathbf{F} = \gamma\alpha(-\hat{S}_{x} \mathbf{i} + \hat{S}_{z} \mathbf{k})
\end{align*}
$$
The $x$-component is averaged out due to [[Larmor precession]], while the $z$-component will split the beam into $2s+1$ streams due to quantized spin.[^2018]

[^2018]: 2018\. [[Sources/@griffithsIntroductionQuantumMechanics2018|Introduction to quantum mechanics]], §4.4.4, pp. 174–175
