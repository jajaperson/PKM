[[Function space]]
# Supremum metric
The supremum metric is the metric induced by the [[Uniform norm]] of a [[Function space]] of bound functions.
It is the [[Poset#^sup|supremum]] of distances between corresponding values of the two functions.
For bounded functions on the domain $D$
$$
\begin{align*}
d(f,g) = \|f-g\|_{\infty} = \sup \{ \abs{f(x) - g(x)} : x \in D \}
\end{align*}
$$
This defines a [[Metric space]] for sets of real or complex valued [[Continuity|continuous]] functions.

- The supremum metric can be used to define the [[Limits in a function space]].
