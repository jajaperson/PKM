[[Dynamical system]]
# Conservative system

A conservative system is a system for which all infinitesimal volumes remain the same, #m/def/dynamics 
i.e. $\Tr (Df(\mathbf{x})) = 0$ everywhere for a [[differential system]] or $\det(Df(\mathbf{x}))=0$ everwhere for an [[iterated map]].

## Properties

- Often have homoclinic orbits
- Never have stable [[Fixed point|fixed points]]

## Example

- Ideal pendulum — centres (at bottom) and saddles (at top), (homoclinic?) saddles form separatrix between oscillation and rotation.
