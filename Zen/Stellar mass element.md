# Stellar mass element

Assuming a star is spherically symmetric,  we may divide it into shells with uniform density.
These shells are further divided into approximately cube-shaped **stellar mass elements** $dm$ given by
$$
\begin{align*}
dm = \rho(r) \,  dA \, dr
\end{align*}
$$
where $\rho(r)$ is the density at radius $r$. The mass contribution $dM(r)$ from a shell at radius $r$ is thence
$$
\begin{align*}
dM(r) = 4\pi r^2 \rho(r) \, dr
\end{align*}
$$
which gives the **stellar mass gradient**
$$
\begin{align*}
\frac{dM}{dr}(r) = 4\pi r^2 \rho(r)
\end{align*}
$$
