# Homotopy MOC

<dfn>Homotopy theory</dfn> originated as a subfield of [[Topology MOC|topology]]
which is concerned with [[Homotopy invariant|homotopy invariants]]  rather than [[Topological property|topological properties]].

1. Originally this meant studying [[Naïve homotopy category]],
    where an object is a [[Topological space]] and a morphism is a [[Homotopy of continuous maps|homotopy class]] of continuous maps. ^1
2. Focusing instead on [[Weak homotopy equivalence of continuous maps]], we get the [[Homotopy category of topological spaces]] as the corresponding [[Homotopy category]].
    This is equivalent to the full subcategory of [[naïve homotopy category]] spanned by [[CW complex|CW complexes]] by the [[Cellular approximation theorem]]. ^2
3. The homotopy theory described in [[#^2]] is really about an induced [[(1,∞)-category]], the [[homotopy category]] being a truncation of this.
    Thus we can generalize homotopy theory to the study of $(1,\infty)$-categories ([[Higher category theory MOC]]).
4. A $(1,\infty)$-category can be presented by a [[Category with weak equivalences]].
    It is nicer when this presenting category has the structure of a [[Model category]].
