[[Charge screening]]
# Colour charge screening
**Colour charge screening** is a form of screening arising from the [[Strong interaction]].
Similarly to the case of [[Electric charge screening]],
[[Pair generation]] will produce virtual qq̄ pairs which screen colour charge.
However, unlike in the electric case,
the virtual gluons also possess colour charge,
and have an _anti-screening_ effect.
Since virtual gluon loops dominate quark loops,
apparent colour charge _increases_ with distance from a bare quark.
This leads to two phenomena:

  1. **Asymptotic freedom** — Quarks are almost free particles at short distances.
  2. **Very rapid colour charge increase** at distances greater than 1 Fermi ($\pu{ E-15m }$). 
     The energy involved "saturates" the colour force 
     and new quarks are created by this energy to restore colour neutrality (see below). 
     It is this that effectively makes the strong interaction short range
