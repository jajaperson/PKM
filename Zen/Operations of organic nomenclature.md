[[Nomenclature of organic chemistry]]
# Operations of organic nomenclature

The basis of [[Nomenclature of organic chemistry#Substitutive nomenclature]] is formed by <dfn>formal operations</dfn>:
Reversible operations which modify the structure of a molecule.
When there is ambiguity as to where the operation might occur,
the relevant morpheme in the name is preceded by a [[Locant in organic nomenclature|locant]].

This Zettel is not (yet) exhaustive, see the [[@favreNomenclatureOrganicChemistry2013|Blue Book]].

## Operations

### Substitutive operation

We exchange or substitute hydrogen atom(s) for another atom or [[Organic group|group]],
applying an affix corresponding to this group.[^P-13.1]

- $\ce{ CH3-CH3 }$ (ethane, <abbr title="preferred IUPAC name">PIN</abbr>) + [[thiol group]] → $\ce{ CH3-CH2-SH }$ (ethanethiol, <abbr title="preferred IUPAC name">PIN</abbr>).
- $\ce{ CH4 }$ (methane, <abbr title="preferred IUPAC name">PIN</abbr>) + $\ce{ 2Cl }$ → $\ce{ CH2Cl2 }$ (dichloromethane, <abbr title="preferred IUPAC name">PIN</abbr>)

### Subtractive operation

We remove an atom or [[Organic group|group]] which is implicit in the name of a compound.[^P-13.4]
Note:

- A <dfn>monohydrogen</dfn> $\ce{ H^. }$ is a neutral hydrogen.
- A <dfn>hydron</dfn> $\ce{ H+ }$ is a hydrogen cation.
- A **hydride** $H^-$ is a hydrogen anion, not be confused with hydride in the sense of [[Parent hydride]].

The syntactic representations vary:

##### Suffix

- The suffix _-yl_ indicates the loss of a monohydrogen to make a [[Free radical]].
    - $\ce{ CH4 }$ (methane, <abbr title="preferred IUPAC name">PIN</abbr>) - $\ce{ H^. }$ (monohydrogen) → $\ce{ CH3^. }$ (methyl, <abbr title="preferred IUPAC name">PIN</abbr>)
- The suffix _-ide_ indicates the loss of a hydron to make an anion.
    - $\ce{ CH3-CH3 }$ (ethane, <abbr title="preferred IUPAC name">PIN</abbr>) - $\ce{ H+ }$ (hydron) → $\ce{ CH3-CH2^-}$ (ethanide, <abbr title="preferred IUPAC name">PIN</abbr>)
- The suffix _-ylium_ indicates the loss of a hydride to make a cation.
    - $\ce{ CH3-(CH2)2-CH3 }$ (butane, <abbr title="preferred IUPAC name">PIN</abbr>) - $H^{-}$ (hyride) → $\ce{ CH3-(CH2)2-CH2^+ }$ (butylium, <abbr title="preferred IUPAC name">PIN</abbr>)

##### Ending change

- Replace _-ic acid_ with _-ate_ to indicate the loss of a hydron to make a cation
    - $\ce{ C6H5-SO2-OH }$ (benzenesulfonic acid, <abbr title="preferred IUPAC name">PIN</abbr>) - $\ce{ H^+ }$ (hydron) → $\ce{ C6H5-SO2-O^{-} }$ (benzenosulfate, <abbr title="preferred IUPAC name">PIN</abbr>)
- Replace _-ane_ with _-ene_ to indicate the loss of two hydrogen atoms in exchange for a double bond on the parent structure. See [[Alkene]].
    - $\ce{ CH3-(CH2)2-CH3 }$ (butane, <abbr title="preferred IUPAC name">PIN</abbr>) - $\ce{ 2 H }$ → $\ce{ CH3-CH2-CH=CH2 }$ (but-1-ene, <abbr title="preferred IUPAC name">PIN</abbr>)
