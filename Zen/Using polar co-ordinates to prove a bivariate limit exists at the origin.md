[[Multivariable limits]]
# Using polar co-ordinates to prove a bivariate limit exists at the origin.
In order to prove a limit at $\vtwo{0}{0}$ exists for a function of form $f: D \to \mathbb R$,
where $D \sube \mathbb R^2$,
we must show that the limit is the same from all directions of approach.
In order to make this easier,
we can convert the input from cartesian form $\vtwo x y$ to polar form $(r, \theta)$,
where $r \in [0, \infty)$ and $\theta \in [0, 2\pi)$.
Since $(x, y) \to (0, 0)$ corresponds to $r \to 0$,
it is only necessary to show the limit of the converted function as $r \to 0$
_regardless of $\theta$_.
Often this will involve the [[Squeeze Theorem]].
