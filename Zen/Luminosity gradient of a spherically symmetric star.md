# Luminosity gradient of a spherically symmetric star

In the spherically symmetric model, the [[Luminosity]] gradient obeys the ODE
$$
\begin{align*}
\frac{dL}{dr} = 4\pi r^2 \rho(r) \epsilon(r)
\end{align*}
$$
where $\epsilon(r)$ is the power per unit mass produced at radius $r$.
Additionally, it can be shown that [[irradiance]] through the sphere of radius $r$ is proportional to the temperature gradient
$$
\begin{align*}
F(r) &= -\lambda \frac{dT}{dr} \\
L(r) &= -4\pi r^2 \lambda \frac{ dT}{dr}
\end{align*}
$$
where $\lambda$ is conductivity, which can be related to [[Opacity]] by
$$
\begin{align*}
\lambda = \frac{4acT^3}{3\rho\kappa}
\end{align*}
$$
where $a = 4\sigma / c = \pu{ 4.5657E-16 J.m-3.K-4}$ is the radiation density constant.
