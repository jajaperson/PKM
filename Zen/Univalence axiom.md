[[Homotopy type theory]]
# Univalence axiom

For a given [[Universe type|universe]] $\mathcal{U}$, the <dfn>univalence axiom</dfn> $\mathrm{ua}_{\mathcal{U}}$ witnesses that the obvious map
$$
\begin{align*}
\mathrm{idtoequiv}_{\mathcal{U}} : (A \equiv_{\mathcal{U}} B) \to (A \simeq B)
\end{align*}
$$
is in fact an [[Typal equivalence|equivalence]].
When $\mathcal{U}$ has such a witness it is called <dfn>univalent</dfn>.[^1]
