[[Dimension of a vector space]]
# Finite-dimensional vector space

Constructively, a $\mathbb{K}$-[[vector space]] $V$ has dimension $n$ if there merely exists an isomorphism $V \cong_{\mathbb{K}} \mathbb{K}^n$,
i.e. $V$ is [[Free module|$n$-framable]]. #m/def/linalg 
Thus a <dfn>finite-dimensional vector space</dfn> $V$ is equipped with an $n$ such that $V$ is $n$-dimensional, i.e. an element of
$$
\begin{align*}
\coprod_{n \in \mathbb{N}} \|V \cong_{\mathbb{K}} \mathbb{K}^n \|.
\end{align*}
$$
