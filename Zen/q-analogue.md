# $q$-analogue

Let $n \in \mathbb{Z}^+$.
The <dfn>$q$-analogue</dfn> $(n)_{q}$ of $n$ is defined by
$$
\begin{align*}
(n)_{q} = \frac{q^n - 1}{q-1} = \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} q^i
\end{align*}
$$
where we note that $\lim_{q \to 1 } (n)_{q} = n$.
The $q$-analogue of other functions is given by replacing $n$ with $(n)_{q}$, see e.g. [[Gaußian binomial coëfficient]].
