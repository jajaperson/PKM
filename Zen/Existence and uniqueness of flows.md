# Existence and uniqueness of continuous flows

Let $F : \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}^n$ be [[Differentiability|differentiable]].
Then for each $x_{0}\in \mathbb{R}^n$
there exists $T > 0$ 
such that $\dot{x} = F(x)$ has a unique solution $q(t,x_{0})$ for $t \in (-T,T)$ with $q(0,x_{0})=x_{0}$.[^2021]
