# Hopf algebra

Let $\mathcal{K}$ be a commutative ring. A <dfn>Hopf $\mathcal{K}$-algebra</dfn> #m/def/ralg/hopf is a [[Hopf monoid object|Hopf monoid]] in [[Category of modules over a commutative ring]].
Thus it is a [[R-bimonoid]] $\mathcal{H}$ equipped with an antipous $\sigma : \mathcal{H} \to \mathcal{H}$,
where in [[R-comonoid#Sweedler notation]] the [[Hopf monoid object#^antipous]] reads
$$
\begin{align*}
\sum_{(x)} \sigma(x_{(1)}) x_{(2)} = \eta \epsilon = \sum_{(x)} x_{(1)} \sigma(x_{(2)}).
\end{align*}
$$
