# Virtual particle
A **virtual particle** is a particle that resembles its real counterpart, but does not obey the standard relativistic [[Energy-momentum relation]]
$$
\begin{align*}
E_\text{classical} = \sqrt{ p^2c^2+m^2c^4 }
\end{align*}
$$
or its non-relativistic form
$$
\begin{align*}
E_\text{newtonian} = \frac{p^2}{2m}
\end{align*}
$$
and has its existence limited by the [[Uncertainty principle]]
$$
\begin{align*}
\Delta E\,\Delta t \leq \frac{\hbar}{2}
\end{align*}
$$
where $\Delta E = \abs{E - E_\text{classical}}$ is how much a particle's actual energy varies from its classical energy. **Real particles** are therefore particles with $\Delta E=0$ wherefore they can exist indefinitely.
This is closely related to the concept of [[On-shell and off-shell]].

Virtual particles play an important role in particle physics,
where virtual gauge bosons act to mediate [[Fundamental interactions]].
