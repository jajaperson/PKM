# $\alpha$- and $\omega$-limit sets

The **$\omega$-limit set** $L_{\omega}(x_{0})$ (**$\alpha$-limit set** $L_{\alpha}(x_{0})$) of $x_{0} \in M$ is the set of all $x \in M$ for which there exists a strictly increasing (decreasing), unbounded sequence of times $(t_n)_{n=1}^\infty$ such that $\lim_{ n \to \infty } \Phi^{t_{n}}x_{0} = x$.[^2021] #m/def/dynamics 

[^2021]: 2021\. [[Sources/@walkerMATH3021NonlinearDynamics2021|MATH3021: Nonlinear dynamics & chaos]], pp. 54ff.

## Properties

1. If $\Phi^t$ is bounded then $L_{\omega}(x_{0})$ is inhabited and connected
2. If $L_{\omega}(x)$ is closed then it contains its limit points
3. $L_{\omega}(x)$ is [[Invariant set]]
4. $z \in L_{\omega}(y)$ and $y \in L_{\omega}(x)$ implies $z \in L_{\omega}(x)$
