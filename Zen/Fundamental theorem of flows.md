# Fundamental theorem of flows

Let $F : \mathbb{R}^n, \mathbb{R}^n$ be differentiable.
Let $x_{0} \in \mathbb{R}^n$,
$U$ be a neighbourhood of $x_{0}$,
$F(x) \neq 0$ for $x \in U$, 
and $v \in \mathbb{R}^n$, $v \neq 0$.
Then there exists a differentiable change of coördinates $y = \Psi(x)$
such that $\dot{x} = F(x)$ is equivalent to $\dot{y} = v$ for $x \in U$.[^2021] #m/thm/dynamics/flow

the only interesting behaviour occurs in neighbourhoods of fixed points.
