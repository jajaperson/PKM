# Manifold

In essence, an $n$-dimensional **manifold** is some kind of space that _locally_ resembles $n$-dimensional [[real coördinate space]].
There are multiple levels to this concept.
A fundamental role is played by [[Coördinate chart|charts]] gathered in an [[atlas]] which describe this resemblance,
and [[Transition map|transition maps]] between charts.

- A [[Topological manifold]] is the most basic kind of manifold, consisting of spaces that locally resemble $\mathbb{R}^n$ topologically.
  Every manifold is a topological one.
- A [[Differentiable manifold]] or $C^\alpha$-manifold has globally defined differential structure, i.e. their transition maps are $\alpha$-differentiable.
- A [[Holomorphic manifold]] is an analytic manifold which locally resembles $\mathbb{C}^n$ with holomorphic transition maps.


Often manifolds are required to be [[Hausdorff space|Hausdorff]] and [[Paracompact space|paracompact]].
