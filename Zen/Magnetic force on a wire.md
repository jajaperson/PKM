# Magnetic force on a wire
A wire with a current $I$ flowing through it experiences a [[Lorentz force law|Lorentz force]] given by
$$
\begin{align*}
\vab F = \int (I\,d\vab\ell \times \vab B) 
\end{align*}
$$
which in the case of a straight wire of constant current in a uniform magnetic field simplifies down to
$$
\begin{align*}
\vab F = I \vab\ell \times \vab B
\end{align*}
$$
Combining this with [[Magnetic field from a long straight wire]],
we can derive the [[Force between two straight current carrying wires]].
