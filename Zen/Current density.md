# Current density

The **volume current density** $\vab J : \pu{{A.m-2}}$ is a vector density defined as
$$
\begin{align*}
\vab J = \rho_{+} \vab v_{+} + \rho_{-} \vab v_{-}
\end{align*}
$$
where

- $\rho_{\pm}$ is the [[charge density]] restricted to positive or negative charges respectively.
- $\vab v_{\pm}$ is the [[velocity field]] of of positive or negative charges respectively.

The current $\dj I$ through a surface element $d \vab a$ is then
$$
\begin{align*}
d I = \vab J \cdot d\vab a
\end{align*}
$$
