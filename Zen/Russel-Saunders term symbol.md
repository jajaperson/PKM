[[Fine structure]]
# Russel-Saunders term symbol

A **Russel-Saunders term symbol** is conventional notation for a [[Fine structure]] energy level, due to [[LS-coupling]].
A term (or level) symbol has the general form
$$
\begin{align*}
{^{2S+1}L_{J}}
\end{align*}
$$
where $L = S,P,D,F\dots$ is denoted in spectroscopic notation.
Technically, a **term** $^{2S+1}L$ includes only $L$ and $S$ 
and splits into $(2S+1)(2L+1)$ **levels** $^{2S+1}L_{J}$ once fine structure is applied.
Each level consists of $2J+1$ **states** (differing by $M_{J}$),
the energies of which may be split by [[Hyperfine structure]].

The validity (i.e. conservation) of the quantum numbers in term symbols decreases with increased atomic number as the effects of [[jj-coupling]] take over.

The term symbol for the ground state of a neutral atom in the $s$ and $p$ blocks is determined by [[Hund's rules]].
