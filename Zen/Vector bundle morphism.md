# Vector bundle morphism

Let $\pi : E \twoheadrightarrow X$ and $\pi' : E' \twoheadrightarrow X$ be [[vector bundle|vector bundles]] over a common base space $X$ in a category $\cat C$.
A <dfn>vector bundle morphism</dfn> $f : E \to E'$ is a map $f \in \cat C(E,E')$ such that #m/def/geo/diff 

![[bundle-map.svg#invert|https://q.uiver.app/#q=WzAsMyxbMCwwLCJFIl0sWzIsMCwiRSciXSxbMSwyLCJYIl0sWzAsMSwiZiJdLFswLDIsIlxccGkiLDIseyJzdHlsZSI6eyJoZWFkIjp7Im5hbWUiOiJlcGkifX19XSxbMSwyLCJcXHBpJyIsMCx7InN0eWxlIjp7ImhlYWQiOnsibmFtZSI6ImVwaSJ9fX1dXQ==]]

commutes in $\cat C$ and the restriction $f \restriction E_{p} : E_{p} \to E'_{p}$ to each fibre is an $\mathbb{R}$-[[linear map]].
Thus being a vector bundle morphism is a stronger property than being a [[bundle map]].
Vector bundle morphisms are the morphisms of [[Category of vector bundles]].
